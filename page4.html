Final Thoughts

Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki holds a very special place in my heart because of the lessons it teaches about the importance of financial intelligence. Being in a family filled with entrepreneurs, yet also seeing in my own immediate family the "poor dad" mindset, I've realized the importance of understanding American economy, personal finance, asset models, liabilities, and the market. This book, being the first book on Personal Finance I've read, has had an amazing impact on my overall understanding on the subject and has pushed me towards looking into investing income generating assets and plan for an early retirement. Although I'm doing my best to implement all of the lessons taught in this book, my progress compared to a year ago where I had no idea what personal finance even was is greatly improved. I'd recommend reading this book with an open mind and to criticize, reflect upon, and try understanding Roberts point of view as much as you can.
