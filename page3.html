My 5 Key Takeaways

Assets vs. Liabilities

The simplest way of understanding this is by thinking of assets as things that actively put money in your pocket while liabilities actively take money out of your pockets.

It's Not Wrong To Work A Job

Working a job isn't what Robert is cautioning against, rather he cautions against no building assets using the income you recieve. In a flow chart, he showed how income from a job should first be used to build your asset column, and then pay the bills.

Pay Yourself First

It's important to be financially responsible, and part of that is to always pay yourself first. That means you should be building your asset column first (paying yourself first) and then paying your bills.

It's Okay To Have Lifes Luxuries...but...

It's wise to invest in assets and income generating streams to pay off your luxuries (liabilities).

Give To Charity

The richest people always donate in charity and give back their money as a practice. There is a common saying: "Give and you shall recieve". In a world of transactions, giving will always result in reception.
